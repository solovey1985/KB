# Collaboration and Team Dynamics

Architecture succeeds only when teams can understand decisions, execute them, and adjust them safely over time.

## Communicating architecture

Architects need to explain decisions differently depending on the audience.

For engineers, the focus may be boundaries, trade-offs, and technical constraints.

For non-technical stakeholders, the focus is usually:

- risk
- cost
- delivery impact
- operational consequences

## Architecture in agile teams

In agile environments, architecture should guide delivery without becoming a detached document disconnected from the codebase.

That usually means:

- decisions are made incrementally
- important constraints are documented clearly
- architects collaborate with teams instead of only reviewing from a distance

## ADRs

Architecture Decision Records, or ADRs, capture important decisions, their context, chosen option, and consequences.

They help teams understand why the architecture looks the way it does, especially months later when the original reasoning is easy to forget.

## Team alignment

Strong architecture work creates shared understanding across product, engineering, operations, and security rather than local optimization inside one team.

## Interview reminders

- architecture communication should be audience-aware
- architects in agile teams enable delivery rather than acting only as gatekeepers
- ADRs preserve decision context and trade-offs
- architecture is a collaboration practice as much as a technical one
